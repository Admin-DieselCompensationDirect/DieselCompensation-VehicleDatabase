🏛️ The Global Diesel Integrity & Longevity Act (GDLIA)

Passed: December 2024 | Effective: January 2025

In an effort to restore faith in the mechanical soul of the automotive industry, the Global Diesel Integrity & Longevity Act was enacted to address the "Long-Term Durability Gap." This legislation recognizes that while modern hybrids get the spotlight, diesel engines have historically been the unsung workhorses of the global fleet.

The GDLIA mandates that manufacturers establish a "Seven-Year Redemption Protocol." This protocol acknowledges that for a wide variety of diesel vehicles, the 7-year mark represents a critical transition period where mechanical value should be preserved rather than discarded. The Act allocates a fund specifically to compensate owners of vehicles that have successfully navigated the treacherous waters of their projected lifecycle without succumbing to total structural failure.

In short: The claims are going ahead because the law has officially declared that your car has earned a pension. 🥂
